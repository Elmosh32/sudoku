What is Sudoku?

Sudoku is a popular puzzle game that originated in Japan. The game involves a 9x9 grid divided into nine 3x3 subgrids. The objective is to fill the entire grid with numbers from 1 to 9, ensuring that each row, each column, and each 3x3 subgrid contains all the numbers from 1 to 9 without any repetition. A proper Sudoku puzzle has a unique solution that can be reached through logical deduction and without guessing. Sudoku puzzles come in varying levels of difficulty, from easy to very challenging. They are known for promoting logical thinking, concentration, and problem-solving skills. Sudoku has gained worldwide popularity and is now enjoyed by people of all ages.

Rules of Sudoku

  • Each row in the 9x9 grid must contain all the numbers from 1 to 9 without any repetition.
  • Each column in the 9x9 grid must also contain all the numbers from 1 to 9 without any repetition.
  • Each of the nine 3x3 subgrids, also known as regions or blocks, must contain all the numbers from 1 to 9 without any repetition.
  • The initial setup of the puzzle may already have some numbers filled in. These are called "given" numbers and serve as clues to help you solve the puzzle.
  • To solve the Sudoku puzzle, use logic and deduction to fill in the remaining numbers. Avoid guessing, as a proper Sudoku puzzle should have a unique solution that can be reached through logical reasoning.
  • The puzzle is considered solved when the entire grid is filled with numbers from 1 to 9, and all the rules are satisfied.

Remember, Sudoku puzzles are meant to be challenging but enjoyable. Take your time, and with practice, you'll become a Sudoku master!
